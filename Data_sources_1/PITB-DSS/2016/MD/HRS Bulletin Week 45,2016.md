## CASE DEFINITION

Dog bites or dog attacks are attacks on humans by stray or domestic dogs. Certain breeds of dogs may be more notorious for such attacks. Millions of people are affected globally each year. Most bites occur in children and most bites are from the pets of family or friends. A person bitten by the animal is at risk of wound infection and tetanus. Dogs are the main source of transmission of rabies to humans which is potentially 100% fatal. Rabies results in the death of approximately 55,000 people a year globally with most of them due to dog bites. Rabies can be prevented by pre-exposure and post-exposure vaccination to humans and vaccination of the domestic animals. Control of stray dog population is the only effective public health measure to curtail this deadly disease. Dog Bite Victims Centre in IPH Lahore provides vaccination support to more than 2000 people from all over Punjab each month.
