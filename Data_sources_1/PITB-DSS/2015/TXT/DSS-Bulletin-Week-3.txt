 CASE DEFINITION OF MALARIA
Suspected case of uncomplicated Malaria: History of recent fever (may be continuous or irregular in beginning),
chills, headache, body aches, weakness, anaemia, hepato-splenomegaly.
 In falciparum infection, the fever may be continuous with bouts of high peaks.
 Suspected case of severe/complicated Malaria: H/O fever with prostration (inability to sit), altered consciousness
 (lethargy, coma), generalized seizures (followed by coma), difficulty in breathing, low urinary output or dark urine,
severe anaemia, abnormal bleeding, and hypoglycaemia.
 n Malaria candevelop into severe Malaria if not treated promptly especially in children and
 Only Falciparum
pregnant women.
