CASE DEFINITION
"Any person with acute febrile illness of > 3 days and <10 days, platelets < 100,oo0, and any one of the following
Hemorrhagic or purpuric rash, vomiting with blood, cough with blood, blood in stools, epistaxis, or other hemorrhagic signs.
  Note: Initially Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (VHF) cases in Pakistan include both Crimean Congo Hemorrhagic Fever (CCHF) and
  Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF), (Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever definition also required hematocrit rise ≥ 20% previously) but
 after 2013 Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) was excluded from the Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (VHF) criteria, but after 2013 VHF
includes only CCHF. According to WHO the term Viral Hemorrhagic Fever include diseases Like Ebola and Marburg Fever, Yellow
  Fever, Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic Fever, Hantaan hemorrhagic Fever, Lassa Fever, Junín Fever, Machupo Fever, Rift Valley
Fever, Omsk hemorrhagic fever and Kyasanur forest disease.
