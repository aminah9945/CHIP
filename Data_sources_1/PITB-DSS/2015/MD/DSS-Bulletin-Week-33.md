## CASE DEFINITION

"Any person with acute febrile illness of > 3 days and <10 days, platelets < 100,000, and any one of the following: Hemorrhagic or purpuric rash, vomiting with blood, cough with blood, blood in stools, epistaxis, or other hemorrhagic signs.
Note: Initially the case definition of Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (VHF) as used on DSS Dashboard included both Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) and Crimean Congo Hemorrhagic Fever (CCHF). In 2013, Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) was excluded from Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (VHF) and entered as a separate category, thereby leaving CCHF alone. However, according to WHO the term Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (VHF) also includes other diseases like Ebola Virus Disease, Marburg Fever, Yellow Fever, Hantaan Hemorrhagic Fever, Lassa Fever, Junin Fever, Machupo Fever, Rift Valley Fever, Omsk Hemorrhagic Fever and Kyasanur Forest Disease.
