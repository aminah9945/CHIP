CASE DEFINITION
                      Suspected case of uncomplicated malaria: History of recent fever (may be continuous or irregular in beginning), chills,
headache, bodyaches, weakness, anaemia, hepato-splenomegaly
In falciparum infection, the fever may be continuous with bouts of high peaks.
                   Suspected case of severe/complicated malaria: H/O fever with prostration (inability to sit), altered consciousness (lethargy,
coma), generalized seizures (followed by coma), difficulty in breathing, low urinary output or dark urine, severe anaemia,
abnormal bleeding, and hypoglycaemia.
Only falciparum malaria can develop into severe malaria if not treated promptly especially in children and pregnant women.
