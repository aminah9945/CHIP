Public Health Actions




                              Public Health Actions

   Federal, Provincial, Regional Health Departments and relevant programs may consider following public
   health actions to prevent and control diseases.

   Gonorrhea

    •  Enhance Case Detection and Reporting: Strengthen the implementation of gonorrhea

       surveillance within IDSR by training healthcare providers on case definitions, diagnostics, and

       reporting protocols.

    •  Address Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR): Establish routine testing for antibiotic susceptibility in

       gonorrhea cases to monitor resistance trends and guide treatment protocols.

    •  Community Awareness: Leverage local health workers and community influencers to hold

       community awareness sessions.

   Rubella

    •  Enhance Case Detection and Reporting: Strengthen rubella surveillance within IDSR by

       integrating case-based reporting and ensuring laboratory confirmation, especially for congenital

       rubella syndrome (CRS).

    •  Strengthen Immunization Coverage: Expand measles-rubella (MR) vaccination coverage through

       routine immunization and periodic supplementary immunization activities (SIAs) targeting

       children and women of childbearing age.

    •  Expand Public Health Education: Promote awareness about rubella, its complications, and the

       importance of vaccination, particularly among parents, caregivers, and healthcare providers.
