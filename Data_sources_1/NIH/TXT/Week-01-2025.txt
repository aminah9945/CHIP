Public Health Actions




   Public Health Actions



   Federal, Provincial, Regional Health Departments and relevant programs may consider following public
   health actions to prevent and control diseases.



             ALRI in children < five years

        x     Enhance Surveillance: Strengthen the surveillance of ALRI cases at public health facilities and
              incorporate data from private sector as well, especially during flu seasons.
        x     Strengthen Lab Systems: Enhance the capacity of laboratory systems to easily detect the circulating
              strains in the population.
        x     Promote Hygiene Practices: Launch health education campaigns on proper respiratory hygiene
              (Covering coughs, frequent hand washing).
        x     Enhance vaccination: Vaccination in high-risk groups (asthmatics, children < 5) for ALRI is advised.

        HIV/AIDS

   x         Expand HIV Screening and Testing: Increase access to screening and testing, particularly in high-risk
             populations.
   x         Ensure Access to ART: Provide immediate access to antiretroviral therapy (ART) for individuals living
             with HIV.
   x         Strengthen Community Awareness Programs: Continue community awareness and health education
             programs, focusing on high-risk populations and areas for prevention of HIV/AIDS, including counseling
             regarding stigmatization associated with the disease.
