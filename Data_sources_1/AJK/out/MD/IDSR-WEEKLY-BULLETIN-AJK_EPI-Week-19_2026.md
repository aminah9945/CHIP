## Highlights

* The overall reporting compliance rate stands at 99.8 %, indicating strong adherence.

* During Epi week 19, the most frequently reported cases were of Acute Diarrhea (Non-Cholera) followed by ILI, Pneumonia/ALRI, Severe Acute Respiratory Infection, Acute Viral Hepatitis (A & E), Animal / Dog Bite and Tuberculosis.

* Among Water / Food Borne Diseases, Acute Diarrhea (Non-Cholera) cases have increased this week.

* No death reported during this week.

* 01 case of Diphtheria (probable) reported from district Muzaffarabad.

* 140 cases of Acute Viral Hepatitis (A & E) reported this week.

* 138 cases of Animal / Dog Bite reported during Epi week 19.

* 02 cases of Acute Flaccid Paralysis (01 from MZD & 01 from Mirpur) have been reported during this week.

Photograph of a training session with healthcare workers

A comprehensive training session on Dengue surveillance, prevention, and response was conducted by Dr. Syeda Amina Batool, Surveillance Officer IDSRS at THQ Hajira for frontline healthcare workers, including Junior Technicians CDC, LHWs, and LHSs.

Photograph of an awareness workshop with female healthcare staff

The DHO Office, Bagh, conducted an awareness workshop on dengue and malaria for LHS and CDC staff. The session was conducted by the In-Charge, CDC Sector Bagh, and Dr. Anees, Health Education Officer, IDSRS, DHO Office Bagh.
