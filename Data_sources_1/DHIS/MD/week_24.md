## Summary:

In this report, different indicators of health facilities have been discussed and the data of teaching/tertiary care hospitals is also included. The detailed analysis of week 24 of year 2022 is being presented in this report, the daily reporting regularity of Rawalpindi and Mianwali approximately 100% whereas district Sialkot, Hafizabad, Okara, Jhang, Khanewal, Jhang, D.G Khan, Pakpattan, Muzaffargarh and Bhakkar have far below reporting compliance for the week 24 of year 2022.

The total OPD Visits during week 24 of 2022 were approximately 2 million (1,984,105) out of which 694K (694,265) were reported in BHU(all), 328K (327,523) in RHC (all), 486K (486,062) in THQ (all), 253K (252,686) in DHQ(all) and 224K (223,569) were reported in Teaching hospitals.

Sixty diseases are reported through DHIS-2 in 35 districts of Punjab at where DHIS-2 has implemented successfully.

Antenatal care coverage which is an indicator of access and utilization of RMNCH services regarding pregnancy has been analyzed.

During week 24, the overall ANC-1 reported coverage in 35 districts of Punjab was 68,944 of the total expected population (3.4%). Overall, the highest number of visits of ANC-1 has been recorded in district Faisalabad under the health facility BHU i.e. 2,480 visits. Due to the missing daily RMNCH report of district Gujrat, Sialkot, Faisalabad, the average of per day ANC-1 visits are affected.

Delivery coverage at health facility is an indicator of utilization of delivery services provided at public health facilities. The overall percentage of deliveries conducted in health facilities of 35 districts of Punjab. During week 24 the highest (25%) C-section cases has reported in district Rawalpindi and the highest cases (98%) of normal delivery cases were reported in district Rajanpur.

Indoor patient’s data of week 24 has shown in (fig. 8 & fig. 9) which cleared the statistics of indoor admissions and death.

During week 24, family planning visits reported from the public health facilities against the expected population were 36, 131. The highest number of FP visits were reported in district Muzaffargarh (84%) under the health facility Basic Health Unit.
