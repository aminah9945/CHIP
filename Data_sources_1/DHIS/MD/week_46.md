**Summary:**

In this report, different indicators of health facility are discussed. The data of teaching/tertiary care hospitals is also included. The detailed analysis of week 45 of year 2021 are being presented in this report. The reporting regularity of the district Bahawalnagar, Jhelum, , Rahimyar Khan, Mianwali and Rawalpindi has accurate reporting compliance targets for the week 45 of year 2021. District D.G. Khan, Kasur, Pakpattan, Bhakkar and Attock did not meet the reporting compliance targets completely.

The total OPD Visits during week 45 of 2021 was 721.0K out of which 186.5k were reported in BHU(all), 125.3k were reported in RHC (all), 173.4k were reported in THQ (all), 130.6k were reported in DHQ(all) and 105.2k were reported in Teaching hospitals.

On average, 37 OPD per day visits were reported in BHU’s, 182 per day visits were reported in RHC’s, 803 per day visits were reported in THQ hospitals, 3629 per day OPD visits were reported in DHQ hospitals and 2923 OPD per day visits were reported in Teaching Hospitals. It is calculated as total OPD visits by dividing the number of reporting health facilities and the total number of days excluding Sunday.

Fifty-three diseases are reported through DHIS, out of which eleven prone Epidemic diseases of 14 districts of Punjab on which DHIS-2 is implemented successfully, have been discussed in this report through DHIS-2 software.

Antenatal care coverage is an indicator of access and utilization of health care services during pregnancy.

During week 45, the overall ANC-1 reported coverage in 14 districts of Punjab was 17,777 of the total expected population (3.4%). On average, the highest number of visits of ANC-1 has been recorded in district Okara under the health facility BHU i.e. 1182 visits.

Delivery coverage at health facility is an indicator of utilization of delivery services provided at public health facilities. The overall percentage of deliveries conducted in health facilities of 14 districts of Punjab. During week 45 the highest (27%) C-section cases has reported in district Rawalpindi and the highest cases (99%) of normal delivery cases were reported in district Kaur. This discrepancy in district Kasur is due to not reporting complete data of THQ and DHQ health facilities.

Indoor patient’s data of week 45 has shown in (fig. 8 & fig. 9) which cleared the statistics of indoor admissions and death.

During week 45, family planning visits reported from the public health facilities against the expected population were 10, 450. The highest number of FP visits were reported in district Muzaffargarh (85%) under the health facility Basic Health Unit.
