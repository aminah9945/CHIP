EXECUTIVE SUMMARY


In this report, different indicators of health facility are discussed. The data of teaching/tertiary care hospitals
is also included. The detailed analysis of week 26 of year 2021 are being presented in this report. The overall
reporting compliance of the health facilities in 9 districts of Punjab remained above the target except the
district D.G. Khan, Kasur and Rawalpindi. The total OPD in week 26 of 2021 was 150.6K out of which 16.5K
were reported Bahawalnagar, 14.8K were reported in D.G Khan, 11.8K were reported in Jhelum, 30.4K were
reported in Kasur, 19.0K were reported in Muzaffargarh, 12.5K were reported in Okara, 3.9K were reported
in Pakpattan,27.9K were reported in Rahimyar Khan and 13.5K were reported in district Rawalpindi.

On average, 37 OPD per day visits were reported in BHU’s, 158 per day visits were reported in RHC’s, 735
per day visits were reported in DHQ hospitals, 771 per day OPD visits were reported in THQ hospitals and
4,183 OPD per day visits were reported in Teaching Hospitals. It is calculated as total OPD visits by dividing
the number of reporting health facilities and the total number of days excluding Sunday.

Fifty-four diseases are reported through DHIS, out of which eleven most Epidemic diseases of 9 districts of
Punjab have been discussed in this report through DHIS-2 software.

Antenatal care coverage is an indicator of access and utilization of health care services during pregnancy.
During week 26, the overall ANC-1 reported coverage in 9 districts of Punjab was 14,999 of the total
expected population (3.4%). On average, the highest number of visits of ANC-1 has been recorded in
district Okara under the health facility of Basic Health Unit i.e. 60%. The percentages of ANC-1
visits were calculated as the total ANC-1 visits by health facility wise and divide it to total
population (3.4%) reported in week 26 of year 2021.

Delivery coverage at health facility is an indicator of utilization of delivery services provided at public
health facilities. The overall percentage of deliveries conducted in health facil ities of 9 districts of Punjab.
During week 26 the highest (8%) of C-section cases and the highest cases (42%) of normal delivery cases
were reported in district Jhelum. The percentages of deliveries coverage at health facility were
calculated as the total normal + VF deliveries by health facility wise and divide it to total
population (3.4%) reported in week 26 o f year 2021 and the same procedure for C -section as
well.

The highest number of cases of still births were reported in Bahawalnagar i.e. 7 deaths were recorded
during week 26. The highest number of cases for maternal deaths were recorded in Bahawalnagar i.e. 6
deaths were reported during week 26 of 2021. The highest number of neonatal deaths were recorded in
Kasur i.e. 140 number of deaths in week 26 of year 2021.

During week 26, family planning visits reported from the public health facilities agai nst the expected
population were 7,633. The highest number of FP visits were reported in district Jhelum i.e. 46% visits
under the health facility Basic Health Unit.           The percentages of FP visits at health facility were
calculated as total FP visits by health facility wise and divide it to total population (3.4%) reported
in week 26 of year 2021 and the same procedure for C-section as well.
