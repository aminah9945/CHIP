Comparative Analysis of Top 10 CD and NCD:
In the 17th week of 2024 in Punjab, there was a notable rise in communicable diseases. Most common were Acute Upper Respi‐
ratory Infections (AURI), making up 63% of cases, followed by Diarrhea/Gastrointestinal issues and Scabies at 15% and 10% respec‐
tively. On the other hand, Non‐Communicable Diseases were led by Fever of unknown origin, accounting for 32% of cases. Peptic
Ulcer and Hypertension followed, each at 12% and 10%. These findings are based on data collected from outpatient departments
treating both communicable and non‐communicable diseases during this specific timeframe
