 <u>Top 10 Communicable and Non-Communicable Diseases:</u>
 In Week 46 of 2024 in Punjab, there was a notable incidence of major communicable diseases. Acute Upper Respiratory Infections (AURI) accounted for a significant 66% of cases, signaling
 a concerning trend. Following closely behind were cases of diarrhea/Gastroenteritis issues at 10%, and scabies at 10% of the total cases. Conversely, among Non-Communicable Diseases,
 the major issue was Fever of unknown origin, comprising 32% of cases. This was followed by incidents of Peptic Ulcer and Hypertension, both at 11% to the total caseload. These insights
 were gathered from patients seeking medical attention in outpatient departments for both communicable and non-communicable diseases during this period.
